(third) Please help validate my millenial existence

I don’t really know who I am or what I’m doing in life. 

I am 22 years old and I have a vague grasp on reality, at least as defined on my Tinder profile. When I’m not mired in self-doubt about my own cultural identity, I’m crying to the same playlists as everyone else is on Spotify.

All my pets are gender neutral and even though I own no responsbility over feeding them, I respect their right to protest.

If you haven’t noticed, I am suffering from the side-effects from being an over-protected whiz kid with no talent of his own. 

My mother refused to beat me senseless as a child and with sexual abuse a mandatory login requirement for Tumblr, I can’t quite help but feel drowned in my own sense of pre-pubescent worthlessness.

Though don’t worry about me.

Fucking care you prick. I am a capable millennial who is clearly going insane due to the vast extremities of the internet. 

I don’t have a massive following on Twitter, which makes me sad. I don’t even remember the last time Kanye West decided to tweet me and the pain is self-evident in my digital tears.

With asset prices soaring through the roof and with the back-breaking thought of even committing myself to any kind of labour, I feel trapped in my own little bubble. 

I see no point getting a full-time job, because my precious time is worth more than being a paper pusher in an organisation that doesn’t even allow me to take selfie breaks.

Of course, what I’m experiencing is totally normal, except that it’s not normal. I am more special than you, so please deal.

What would be nice, politically acceptance, in fact, is if there were some kind of official qualification that could indicate how awesome you are without the racist bigotry that seems prevalent in society. 

You know. Some kind of standardised, democrat-funded index where any individual with a Facebook account can validate their existence against the bell curve of other saddos who think they’re better than me.

Who gives a shit about the amount of $ you have in your bank account or the state of your parent’s basement.

Those are not real measures of a person’s self-worth. Human individuality is what sets us apart, guided by a spectrum of colours that many are simply unable to see because of their blind insensitivity towards 

Life should be graded by the number of Facebook likes you receive each day or by all the cool references you can make to Will Ferrell movies. 

If I’m popping 8 anti-depressants each day, I should be rewarded for my courage and each LGBT protest I attend should at least be recognised in my future cover letter I’m keeping reserved for when I become editor of a famous publication. 

I will prove my 9th grade teacher wrong.

Especially when the new Microsoft table makes her redundant. I need my free education. 

Someone please give me my free education.
