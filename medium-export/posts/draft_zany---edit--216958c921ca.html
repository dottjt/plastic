zany. (edit)

It’s incredible to think how irrelevant we are as human beings. 

Quite ironically we try SO damn hard to get our brand and identity noticed, only to tilt our heads like deranged beasts at the thought of being approached by petty strangers with such horrible distaste, drowning like a herd of weakened cattle to the sirens of indecency plaguing our perception. 

I’ve always been obsessed with the ways in which self-delusion can manifest and linger over our rotting desires, transplanting their much-celebrated rush into the small worlds we conjure in our minds. Presumption is a drink; the drink we offer as a calamity to the gullible and misfortunate to tout and expire. 

It’s the clear the internet has failed you. 

While Netflix and human life provide much in the way of mind-expanding entertainment to chew through the disease we call “boredom”, there lies another problem with the triviality bleeding from our skulls. Precisely, where does the curiosity go and where does squander?

We’ve all the collapse happen in real-time, the delicate atrophy feeding off the somewhat prolonged darkness emanating from your childhood, drawing our attention and mimicking the worst in our basic programming. 

PS: I find it difficult to tell whether this is bad writing or a pointless charade of poetry that is as helplessly begin as it is relaxing.
