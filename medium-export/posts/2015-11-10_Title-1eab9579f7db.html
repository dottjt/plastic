Title

There is nothing less original, nothing less pedantic, than the overscrupulous tirade that nurtures and inhabits these words. In their rawest sense, they conspire with an honest treachery that even confusion itself refuses to comprehend.

Does she love you? Does she care?

The celestial linger is a farce amongst the clear possibility that the person you cared for most does not exist.

She is a message lost within the clarity; the hatred you’ve trained yourself to ignore. Even our most pure white lacks the structural integrity necessary to bring you back to the illusions harbouring within.

These daunting symbols, these terrible reminders of hope and faith; they cave into the perpetual wanting, the trickling screams, the desire our hearts care not to pretend.

Like a fresh shower in a countryside far too clean, there is an underlying distaste to the sore madness our beauty echos; the incessant shame of our pitter.

Writers are a beautiful species and yet there is something quite disturbed in our pursuit for vulnerability, like a formal suicide in the name of new tradition; these dribs and drabs are grammar, my dear.

Just don’t expect too much of the things you understand least.

By Julius Reade. on November 10, 2015.
