Hello mind. You win. You always win. 

I’m writing today because that’s what my mind feels compelled to do when trapped. My mind is trapped because it refuses to learn. I’m tired. Exhausted. Possibly deranged. Oh, who even cares. 

When you feel trapped, it does incredibly elaborate things to your brain. It suffocates your ability to think and maintain yourself. You become a useless blob, intent on self-combusting.

You see, what happens is that you wire yourself with an agenda. You tell yourself, quite adamantly: “I must study. If I do not study, then I’m not quite sure what, but I MUST study.” It is a mantra which consumes you to a point where you cannot question it. In fact, writing about this now is actually quite raw to me. I didn’t realise how, almost, suicidal my brain is. 

Maybe that’s the only way to function in this world, by trapping yourself into a kind of commitment where even your own mind cannot fight back. I suppose it goes to show how relentlessly efficient the mind can become when forced into a shadow. The world becomes bleak. In fact, you are bleak, though you don’t quite know it. 

It’s like I have to remind myself how to function and I don’t quite know how I should think about it.
