On the other hand, it is also true that very few people do find the courage to change. They are still stuck and riddled within their own mind games, as if time has stopped and placed an eventually timer on their soul.

I’ve always been a particularly morbid and curious individual, with a slight fascination with the mentally ill. All my friends were severely mentally ill to some extent; either avid drug addicts engulfed in the depression or those so obsessed with body image that they were willing to get plastic surgery (and mind you, this person was a guy, contrary to common stereotype).

Naturally, I was somewhere in-between. An addicted, paranoid, hopeless zombie with an exude for human depravity. I was a true born cynic and I very rarely got along with anyone. Part of me did not want to. I wanted all the hatred for myself.

I was sold on this idea that I needed a mentally ill girlfriend.

I thought that by finding someone like me, I would have a better chance of connecting.

In the end, it turned out to be one of the best (and worst) decisions of my entire life. 


Here’s the thing about mental illness that you don’t realise until after you learn to control it: Everyone is mentally ill.

It’s not until you become self-aware of your own destructive habits, do you also become aware of all the destructive habits of others. 

Of course, it is not that I am not without flaw. I still have a difficult time empathising, however I have also learnt that it is something we learn from life. We are all ignorant souls at our core. 

The true beauty of mental illness in an intimate situation, however, is that there is a level of humility to it. There’s a level of awareness that something is not right, which is an important first step for change. 

Back then, there was just something dull, something unwarranted and predictable of those who I considered normal.

My main reason is that we’re all mentally ill to some respect.
