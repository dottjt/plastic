You can think of a narrative as a story we tell in our mind. Often they remain unconscious because they are relatively harmless, such as “Man, I’m feeling really hungry” or “How beautiful is that sunset?”. 

In a way, we rely on our narratives to make sense of our world. They are based in the agreements and beliefs we make about the world, which provide coherence to the emotions which we feel. 

On a much more harmful level, narratives transpire as “I’m not good enough because I’m not thin” or “I can’t stop thinking about my ex”.
