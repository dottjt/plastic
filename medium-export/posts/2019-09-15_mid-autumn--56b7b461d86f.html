mid-autumn.

You could gauge the intensity in his eyes. Bloodshot and merciless. He had mastered his purpose, and that’s really all there was to it.

Like a trained algorithm of infallible precision, his pupils dotted from input to output, scanning the items one-by-one as if tautologically speaking — it was what his hands were designed to do.

You know, maybe it’s just a different world. Where modes are genetic. Where imagination is literal, and clouding your reality is a vision of survival that barely touches itself at all, as if to suggest self-introspection was a very dirty habit indeed.

Diffused and crystallised into packets of untouched sincerity, the remaining scatters of light faltered itself into uneven rows, corrupting the inherent laws of asinine symmetry we’ve all come to know and love.

And yet, the anxiety was real.

It permeated well-beyond his slick haircut and passionless criminal past, worn directly as an omen of forgiveness on his sleeve. The cake is out of time and the 1’s and 0’s surging through his brain have hopelessly collapsed itself into stacks upon stacks of pre-packaged Hitler Youth.

He grabs my partner’s purse. Well, I guess a machine couldn’t do that? Helpful human. You helpful, human thing.

Bureaucratic is the pace.

He was a nice Christian boy.

A classic ABC of gutter affluence. I mean, when mummy has a credit card, why not go wild?

He’s on the left. I’m the on right. The puzzler is in the middle, trying to cookie-cut our experience with cheap foreign wine and uncanny extradition.

Clearly we hate each other. The film is without pleasure.

Oh, and his wife.

Definitely a Calvinist.

“I think they like me.”

Well, obviously I knew they liked me. I had to be liked, it was the only other way. I step off the curb and my ankle organises itself into a series of numb indecisions. The symptoms persist for weeks.

We got the call and the phone won.

Tight-fitted suits with leaves for crowns. Exorbitant like cheap flannelette. Bruising with a brown only the sun could ever fathom.

It was a bit like walking onto the set of an 80s’ porno, except all the actors were dead. Certainly, I felt like I’d died a little inside.

The house. Well, we loved it.

Unfortunately the price tag did not love us.

Snip. Snip. Snip.

I sat there in a frenzy of emotion, bridled by thoughts of childhood and false endeavour. 15 minutes passed.

I looked like Anthony Green, and it was fantastic.

A renewed sense of passion. Maybe the snap backed.

You relax and you put your head back and it’s like, all of a sudden, the elastic breaks and a beautiful facade reveals itself in dramatic fashion.

It’s dim in here and the brain has a lot to chew, which is why I’ve placed each pedestal close to the next. This one speaks Mandarin. The other knows your address. Oh my, can you imagine the possibility!

I’m ready to kick.

Kick it, brother.
