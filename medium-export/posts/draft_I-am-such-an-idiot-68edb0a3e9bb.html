I am such an idiot 

Recovery is a lot like a tepid game of whack-a-mole. 

All you have to do is whack those moles, yet at some point in life, you start questioning why any serious individual would want to do something so pointless as whack a mole. 

Everyone is doing it. It also seems kind of lame. Then following your inactivity, you begin comparing yourself to others who have continued to whack the moles and can seemingly hit more moles than you.

The hatred. The confusion. The hopelessness.  

Yet no matter what you do, the game is constantly running. Billions of other players, hitting those same damn moles that you just can’t quite convince yourself of. 

I suppose I like to view recovery as a trial and error experience. I doubt anyone actually knows what they’re actually doing, yet we somehow get better and more refined overtime. Ultimately we all just put faith into our own ideas and if others following, then so be it. 

I guess I’d had a bit of a realisation about myself. It’s interesting how many epiphanies that you can have about yourself and yet still discover that you’ve only scrapped the surface.

My biggest issue, at least as recognisable for the time being, is that my mind likes to make assumptions. 

I remember reading The Four Agreements and one of the main tenets is not to make assumptions. Of course, they don’t really explain how, though certainly it became obvious to me sometime this weekend. 

I believe it is a trust issue and I believe it is something that I picked up from my mother. It’s the reason why I’m a prime candidate for U.S. State propaganda and I have a difficult time with Facebook.

The moment I see something, someone more “accomplished” than me, my mind processes it, generalises it and then hooks my consciousness in. I don’t know, maybe it’s something everyone does. 

Certainly I know it’s because my mother does this in her mind on a whole new level. Her mind is like a jaded cage, full of bad experiences that she projects onto the world before her. 

Though the main reason comes down to a lack of communication. It’s like a downward spiral. I don’t tell her anything, which prompts her to make generalisations about what I do, which creates this hate which solidifies this notion that I’m certainly never going to tell you anything in the future.  

Of course, I’m sure it’s much deeper than that. Observing her slew of bad relationships and her Asian parent is probably what closed me up as a child. 

Regardless, this all stems from the fact that she’s incredibly judgemental of what I do tell her. So I stopped. I didn’t want to deal with the emotional abuse.

In my small epiphany, I also realised that regardless of what anyone does, I am responsible for who I am and I should be responsible for who I am. 

What made my first relationship so difficult, was because I felt she wouldn’t tell me anything. With that said, I believe it is because I did the exact same thing as my mother as she has to me, which is almost tragic when I come to think about it.
