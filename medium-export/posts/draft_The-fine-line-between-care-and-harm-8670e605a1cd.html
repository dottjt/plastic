The fine line between motherly care and harm

As a child growing up with a single Asian mother, I existed in an environment where criticism was used as a tool for motivation and self-awareness.

Every comment was seemingly ridden with a slight backhand. It was never “You did a wonderful job. You should be proud.” It was always “You did a wonderful job, however you could, sorry, should have done this better.”

Of course, when you’re young and impressionable, you don’t know what is good or bad, right or wrong. You don’t know what you’re capable of, nor have a gauge for how life should be.

You just go with the flow and if you ever feel shit about yourself or unhappy, then that’s probably just because you’re an inherently shit person.

For those who study psychology, especially as I later found out having dealt with my own personal mental health issues, this kind of bad reasoning develops itself into cynical mindsets.

When you are subjected with pessimistic mindsets , you become one of those terrible

Certainly, I was a very pessimistic child.

I hated everyone. I thought everyone else was stupid. More importantly, I

I developed zero ability to empathise with others and yet there I was, the biggest fucking asshole you would have ever met.

I feel different now. I feel like I’m a lot better person and that I’m not entirely harmful, yet

It wasn’t that my mother didn’t care. She cared and loved me beyond belief and still does to this day. She’s the kind of person that has the best of intentions.

It’s just that she’s a dumb, retarded bitch.

I have nothing against her. I feel incredibly grateful to have the privilege of any kind of family; a family which provides support (perhaps not emotional support).

My mother just isn’t very smart and I don’t believe I can blame her for it.

Maybe most mothers aren’t smart. On the one hand, we can be smart individuals, but terrible mothers. I believe my mother is one of those people.

I think it’s no different to men who are terrible at trading stocks or being fathers. Just in the heat of the moment, your emotions get the better of you and you just crumble like an idiot.

It must have been difficult being a single mother. I think over the years she’s developed a strong distaste for men, or rather, for individuals that are not her.

Part of the issue is that I’ve become my mother and I’ve lost most my friends due to her way of thinking. I have a habit of criticising others, rather than giving them praise where it is due.

Each morning, I listen to the same album by the same band. Circa Survive’s second EP Appendage.

There’s a line that reminds me to remain humble and to remember that I’m no better than anyone else and I think it’s important for us to remember.

There is no reason to feel bad, helpless or hate others and no, my mother isn’t stupid.

“We all go through the same thing, at different times.”

I want to be a positive role model. I want to rise above and show others that what may seem impossible, can be done. That is how I choose to live my life, as someone capable and alive.
