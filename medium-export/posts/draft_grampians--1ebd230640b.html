grampians.

Fickle hair; dreamy wonder. 

The mist breathes like snow, gathering attention amongst the damp wetness shrouding our thoughts. Grand views and frostbite exterior. Welcome to the unforgiving expansion of your mind, the weekend you cherish in front of a gas log fire. 

My girlfriend and I decided to go to the grampians for our anniversary weekend. Four days we stayed in a wooden cabin, retrofit with all the modern complexities I’m sure you’d love to know. Two air-conditioning units, knives to cut fruit, as well as a bed as hard as the rocks we would later climb and conquer. 

The grampians is a sort of mountain range in mid-west Victoria, constructed from millions of years of volcanic activity. It’s evident in the flow of the rock, dripping in the lava once prevalent in the chaotic history escaping the land. To be perfectly honest, I have no idea what I’m writing about, but it’s a convincing and beautiful narrative none-the-less. 

Bringing half the kitchen with us in our small Mercedes car thing, I pledged to cook the whole range of wonderfully hearty meals for this weekend away, as an ode to our ever-growing love. Slowed cooked for hours, of course, especially considering we weren’t paying for either the gas or electricity. (wonderful, ain’t it?)
