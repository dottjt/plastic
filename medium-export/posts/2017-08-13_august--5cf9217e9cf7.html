august.

Today feels… indifferent.

It’s the 13th of August 2017 and there’s so much going on in the world. Yet the hyper-realism is real, and I don’t think I quite care.

Nations on the verge of war. Assets redefining unaffordable. Credit strife well-beyond it’s design. Scandal excessive. Stressed beyond reasonable contention. Towering mega-corporations.

Oh, and how they handle our lives like a bunch of intoxicated river geese, dally on the fine-swine of industrial water caress.

Surreptitious and divide.

It’s both a nightmare, as well as a wonderful leisure in disguise — sour in the blaart candy which distorts our world with a tick-tock twist. Certainly, I doubt any of us will be able to taste the difference.

“Lower those expectations honey, and watch those candles rise.”

My fucking gosh. The internet.

The overload is real, and yet the information is questionable. Almost none of it RELEVANT to our well-being, even in-amongst the TARTARE volume, SPLURGE against a banquet of knowledge and restitution BEGGING us for our ATTENTION.

This despicable entity which both scars and pleasures at a constant rhyme, delegating responsibility to an algorithm even which our brightest minds run clueless to comprehend.

Maybe that’s why I’m a coder.

Surely, my inner curiosity has deceived me into thinking I can decipher this digital mess and restore some sense of sanity to this world. H A HA AH

Certainly, it’s what the machine wants you to think and as I dig deeper into the syntax and binary thinking — I merely find myself facilitating the dirty smudge of electronic evil which powers these strings tangling our souls, suffocating our ability to retain culture beyond the ancient relics of last night’s Snapchat feed.

The change is real, and it is moving exponentially towards something unknown. It is the show with no host, in which we all lose relevance and our blur gathers scum, like a cum stain with bad intentions.

It’s as if our entire notion of reality is being driven by this childish thought that we’ve all been programmed to believe, because we’re all too stupid to unplug ourselves from the gluttony fuelling our daily motion.

Of course, the less-than stellar fact is that we’re adapting. We’re adapting to this machine which controls our lives, because we’re eager to survive — some more, come thrive.

“Kill the animal. Drink the blood. Sell it for more.”

It taught you well.

Certainly, I know you absolutely fucking love it, and I know — oh yes, I fucking know — that you want to live long enough to witness it all end, and watch as your own reality tears you down, and rips into your elemental constitution, relieving you of deprivation which clouds your mind.

Go on.

Fuck you too.

I suppose the interesting thing is that while the machine doesn’t experience fear or insolence, we are expected to compete with it as an equal partner. It’s sort of like that super-model partner you’ll never have, for in this reality we are merely seven out of tens’, and it’s this lack of leverage that sends tingles down my spine.

Thankfully, there’s a forum for that — so thank you machine for your resounding empathy!

Though perhaps the machine is designed to make us into better human beings. To exploit us by teaching us to be more efficient. Smarter, more empathetic, more wildly ambitiously than lice — as we crutch aimlessly to the weakness our hearts profess to us on a daily basis.

Cigarettes. Coffee. Pornography.

Your life certainly does love you well.

It is a journey we are all losing, as we dwindle to the gargantuan might of the information pyramid. Only you just lost your job last week, and now you feel like a half-baked porcupine, with all it’s needles ripped out and assorted for the quill factory.

The death is slow and reassuring.

Certainly, we have no choice, nor is there any dignity in having any choice at this stage. No amount of love, hate or physical exertion to shift the machine away from it’s insistent creation, tumbling around, gathering what little left of the world around us, cusp in the recycle bin of it’s directive.

So here we are.

It’s the 13th of August 2017.

And the gradual decay is here to stay.

By Julius Reade. on August 13, 2017.
