The thing I’m most grateful for

It’s hard being a young person.

On the one hand, everything you do in your younger years sets you up later in life, whether it be via education or the people you associate yourself with.

On the other hand, it’s very difficult to actually see any of your efforts pay off, since everything seems so bloody distant. University takes around three years. Internships and entry level jobs another three on top of that. 

Not to mention, we actually have no idea what we’re doing, nor whether we actually want to become journalists, doctors or lawyers and yet are forced into these dreams as if destiny were a fatal expectation.

That’s also assuming that someone even bothers to look at our resume, which for the hunderds of applicants out there for the one job, is unlikely for many of us. 

Yet in my infinite confusion, I decided to persevere.

Part of it was due to foolishness in believing that it is okay to have dreams. The other part was vaguely understanding that anything else is mere short-sightedness, as much as I’m lead to believe otherwise. 

I didn’t really have any idea what I wanted to do. No one does, however I decided to follow my heart and somehow, everything has just fallen into place.

I have a full-time job in PR and Communications where I get to travel the country and writing articles all day. I interview people from within high levels of government and industry (which isn’t nearly as amazing or glamourous as it sounds).

My educaiton actually paid off, and while it was a difficult position to get into, I’ve realised is not what I want to do as a career. 

I was at a conference recently and I was setting up tables when something struck me. 

Not so much an epiphany, so much as a feeling of gratitude that had been lacking in my self-perception of who I was.

In that moment, I felt grateful to have passion in my life.

It wasn’t the news podcast take took me three hours to produce each morning, the 5+ years spent in community radio, the YouTube channel that I dedicated my life towards, the blogs and writings that I had expressed my soul into.

It was my passion for life and these things that got me to where I am today and it is a gift and a blessing that I realise, many people do not have.

It is the reason why mean people stop pursing their careers. It is the reason why people stagnate: because their dreams do not have passion. 

Part of this slight epiphany came down to the fact that I don’t want to work PR or Communications at all. 

, I had a YouTube channel, I volunteered in a radio station for the blind for three years, I did a whole number of things that almost seems kaput today.



Not because of the job or the career progression, but for the raging passion that I feel inside me, amongst the dreams that carry me in my sleep.

Each and every day, I study computer science
