Food Depression

There’s something less-than-appetising about sitting in the middle of a berate shopping centre, greedily entertaining a chunk of expensive Tasmanian camembert (naturally, the retailer had marked it down 66%), chomping down on an excessively incomplete culinary experience.

Impaired by the crass exterior of stupidity itself, I apparently thought it “appropriate” to simultaneously polarise on a delicate can of tuna lurking on the bench next to me; sporadicly invading my tastebuds like a bad template for feigned attrition, as if forming some kind of holy war against my own fake war ideology.

“Only a real man douses his tuna with enough olive oil to drown a child”, I thought smugly, like an idiot seeking dirt for the sake of intellectual clarity.

Unhappy eating is the worst kind of eating, especially when ellicited by an epilectic duo of unhappy foods, clunking your mood; rattling the ship into a constant stir of riddled motion. I feel sick.

Oh, and let’s not forget the can of sardines I’d tried earlier as a “well, the can kind of looks pretty”, qualified as one of the worst culinary decisions of my entire life.

I can still remember the semi-emulsified disappointment cultivating like dead flies in my mouth, refusing to acknowledge the bitter creaminess sinking deep into my rotting teeth.

Dying for even a hint of liquid to wash down my sorrows, I was immediately struck at my lack of human decency; my idealism refusing to pay for something with taste or glamour; lackey Tuesdays, you fucking twat.

If ever freedom became a bastardised consequence of human fatality, the lunch time corporate experience would have to rank as something close to basic detriment.

As someone who is a) excessively cheap and b) tries to eat paleo, my logic leaves me with the option of huffing as much air as I can before returning back to the office.

Usually I resort to skipping lunch altogether; a brave warrior amongst the brim, fighting for a cause which no one will understand. Maybe I should work as a chef?

The interior of my mouth still feels like a drunkenly murderous graveyard robbery, unaltricious in the calamity that shakes my green heart, however eyeing the possibility that one day I may very well give into the trend and spend those humble dollars to feel even an inch of what it means to be human.

I have my doubts.

I’d eat my smokes if I could, but there’s something dissorient in the addictive paper stew which puffs from my mouth.

By Julius Reade. on November 16, 2015.
