I thought I might try and write about what it’s like to no longer have depression.

It’s an odd thing to write about, because it’s like trying to describe a tree that doesn’t exist. It also largely misclassifies what depression is, as some kind of enhanced tsundre of the mind.

Furthermore, it’s even harder to say when I stopped having depression. The mind doesn’t inherently keep track of what it doesn’t do. In fact, it’s largely oblivious to that which doesn’t exist. 

For example, I haven’t thought about unicorns in ages. Exactly when? 

I cannot be sure. 

Instead, it might help if I talked about the nature of depression and how I personally encapsulated it. 

The most important thing to understand about mental illness is that there’s this intense bias that gets exposed to you. Which is to say that you’ll literally never hear of success stories. If you thought mental illness was a silent killer, sufficiently addressed mental health is even rarer beast. 

After all, while someone with depression may be compelled to express themselves out of desperation, someone who is fully functioning has no desire to express something which does not exist.

So what is depression? Well, that entirely depends on how willing you are to bastardise.
