I’m not entirely sure if I enjoy being a writer.

There’s a painful agony that comes with teaching words to behave like civil servants. As a writer you redefine the world without consequence, painting pictures for the sake of illusion. Such carelessness should be revoked, slandered and unashamedly prepared for the perpetual slaughterhouse that is our creative mind. 

I get a little fuzzy with these words.The true beauty however is that words never lie. Only humans lie, but the words don’t know that, allowing you to suspend your disbelief and engulf yourself into the senseless violence and often contradictory statements primary messages send. 

If you must know, I’m currently lying on the floor. I’m lethargic. I don’t really care for the world at this hour and my eyes are barely keeping awake. At the same time, I’m entirely sure if I want to be excited. I mean, I’ve felt excitement in the past, however it’s just not what I’m feeling. Timeless obligations, tying the strong-willed to a series of endless chains. 

Maybe I just want to be loved. To feel love. To be able to look another person in the eye and recognise the heartless passion, drooping down into their coffee as they congratulate themselves for a wonderful day undone. Yet here I am, a stupidly delirious writer, lying on the floor, typing away into a computer that barely knows who I am. 

Even though I spend more time with my laptop than any other object on earth, I don’t quite give it the respect it deserves. I never hug it. I never dress it up or try and make it look pretty, in spite of the fact that I have personally masturbated, cried and broken down in-front of it hundreds, upon hundreds of times before. 

Rather, the laptop is a tool to be used and discarded. It is a medium to pretend. 

These are the words of a writer and I apologise for their actions.
