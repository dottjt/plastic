The story of my second father.

I think there was something familiar in the anger and frustration that emanated from my mind. Admittedly I was experiencing a terrible hangover from the night before and in the middle of summer, was suffering like hot wind in world so incredibly empty. 

Tutoring someone’s child is a very surreal experience, certainly something seasoned tutors experience on a daily basis. In a sense, you’re not their mother or father. You can’t force them to do anything. The dynamics, quite naturally, go far deeper than that. 

To add an extra layer of tension, the child’s father had passed away a year earlier. I remember, because I was at the hospital on the day of his death, thinking little of the ensuing moments. Lying in bed, unconscious, he passed away hours later. 

He had two children at his time of death in late 2014. One was 8 and the other was 10. I was with the children at the hospital playing a jolly game of Smash bros on the hospital GameCube. It was the first time I’d actually ever really met or hung out with his children and they sure were a cheeky bunch. 

The children were in grade 3 and 5 respectively. I was shocked to be informed of two rape incidents that had happened at their school; one incident where three boys in grade three had raped another girl and an instance where a girl in grade one was caught giving a blow job to another boy from grade one.

Gee.

Their father, in a very real sense, was my father. While we weren’t biologically related, a large part of my childhood was spent living with him and I called him dad and truly meant it. Frequently moving around as a child, there were moments where I lived with him, 

He felt like it down to my bones. 

My mother was not romantically connected with him. They were just old friends, though who knows what the past contains. Yet that didn’t matter to me. A father is in the actions and responsibilities you take as a person, not the title you hold. 

…and he was the best possible father you could have. He was hard working. In fact, sculpture was his passion and it was his craft. 

Actually, growing up, I technically had two fathers. One was my biological father, the other was him. I still remember that incredibly awkward moment when they both momentarily met. I felt paralysed and didn’t quite know who to call dad, out of offending them both. I’m not even sure my biological father knew about our arrangement.  

To this day, I’m still not quite sure who was more of a father to me. Certainly, there was a cerebral distaste for my biological father. In many ways, he was the opposite to my other father. I don’t think deadbeat is the right word, however he soon disappeared from my life and I haven’t seen him in over 15 years. 

It was very odd growing up with two fathers, as you can imagine. I remember moments in my life where I was living away from my biological father for years at a time, only to live back with him and even after his permanent departure, was a string of eclectic, largely intoxicated men. 

Though Personally speaking, I have a very fluid concept of what a father is. I think I was my own father growing up, teaching myself as I went along. In other ways, my mother was my father and the whole thing is just an inconvenient mess. 

As a talented Chinese sculpture, though I never paid much attention to it. My mother is an artist who has painted all sorts of famous people, which naturally, just blends itself into the incomprehension of life.

In preparation of his funeral, I had to collect a series of works to show and I was astounded at the significant creations that he had managed to conjure. We are talking about massive, 20 metre installations, in every possible material you could think of. 

I was particularly fond of his bronze work. There’s something magnificent in the timeless creation of metal, forever locked away in the cast iron prefecture our minds will never truly understand. 

It was out of the blue that the children’s mother had approached me to tutor one of her children. 

I will be honest and say that I never liked her. She sort of came late to the party. She appeared one day while I was living with dad and he never truly accepted me.
