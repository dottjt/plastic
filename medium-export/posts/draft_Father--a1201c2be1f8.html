Father.

I’m not sure what my father looks like. 

I don’t own any pictures of him and I haven’t seen him since I was eight (or possibly ten, I don’t remember). There are only two pictures of my father that I remember ever seeing. One picture was blue-tacked onto the wall of the attic in my old Kallista house. In this picture, my father is a young boy on the couch, gleefully sitting with his brothers and sisters. The other picture contains what looks like my father with another female, who may have been a girlfriend at the time before meeting my mother. In my mind, the girlfriend sort of looks like a young Sarah Silverman. 

If I had to describe my father, I would probably say he reminds me of the main protagonist in the film The Wog Boy; constantly smoking and drinking like any working-class figure would do. I remember he used to drive a white van (apparently my mother paid for it), complemented by a Cartlon longneck shoved between his thighs, which would immediately be hidden away between his feet at the sign of any police activity.

I suppose the sad thing is that I don’t feel anything for him. I don’t know who he is, what his dreams and hopes are, what makes him happy or sad. I literally don’t think about him at all. I have no connection with him, no desire to what to know him.

Yet part of me still felt compelled to write this.
