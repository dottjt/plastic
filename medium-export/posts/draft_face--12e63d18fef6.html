I have these moments where I don’t quite know what’s going on. Where the world around me feels like this hollow identification I’ve been brought in to examine. 

“…and to the right of me is a plant of some description, which is strikingly green to the point of intrepidation.”

To be quite honest with you, I find life rather dull. You see, in life you’re either terrified or dull. You’re either living on the edge, with a packet of cigarettes in your left shoulder pocket, ready to punch the living shit out the next bordeau creature you see walk past, or you’re staying at home with a knife on the bench-top counter, keenly awaiting that critical moment where stabbing your eyes out becomes the only counter-measure towards addressing boredom.

Humans dream of excess. We dream of chain smoking an entire pouch of lavender clippings, falling into a daze of Carbon Monoxide poisoning, only for most of us feel held back by reality — as if our dreams are somewhat of a burden on the co-exisence of both our happiness and our future. 

Naturally, there are a few things we should all steer away from if we hope to maintain any amount of sanity. God is one of them. Heroin, likewise. 

Constraints. Almost everything is a constraint in the physical world, yet when our minds are latching onto the digital orgasm of how best to splurge amongst the billions of pixels vying for our attention, ranging from the aesthetic to the obtuse, we collapse into a puddle of electric wrinkle and indecision.

I’ve always wondered if I have bipolar disorder or if life is nothing more than the prestiguous fuck-boy, a ritual anti-exercise in how best to approach the bell-curve which has you choking by the neck. 

Balance has always been something I’ve aspired to achieve. It’s what allows me to thrive, to have my cake and eat it twice. The reason I chase it is quite benign, and somewhat nihilistic. 

Put simply, what difference does it honestly make? What fucking difference does it make, whether I help that small child or slaughter him at the curb with a heavy fist and a deliberate marker? 

It doesn’t. It makes no difference at all, yet it highlights something much more empowering. It’s to say that all choices are valid, and this essentially does is normal the inherently ‘difficult’ and ‘tricky’ decisions most of us find troublesome.

You want your balance? 

No fuckin’ worries mate, because I can have it all.
