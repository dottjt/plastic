You are about to read a number of events as described by my friends, from the moment I blacked out. Though firstly, some context. 

I don’t usually drink an excessive amount of alcohol, at least in season. In fact, I’d recently taken a three month break from drinking, as I believe it was an impact on my mental health. However, I was bored and lonely on a Friday night, and with my housemate the only human contact I’d had all day, decided to splurge on the liquor he routinely would bring home with him. 

Tanqueray. The world’s finest gin. It’s appropriately sweet without wanting to choke you down and as someone who likes to drink straight, is a viable prospect for my dying taste buds. He planted down the bottle like a right of passage on the wooden centrepiece, claiming our friendship and demise in one. 

Though really, it was subtle. 

I’d already had a few ciders to myself, which would set the tone of the night to come. We decided to watch episodes of Peep Show on Stan, a kind of bonding we’d subconsciously established over the short few months living together. Within the first minute, we were erriely reminder by how we were exactly those two main characters in the show. 

Mark, an insecure, self-obsessed and utterly incapable “professional” had just woken up to the house being completely trashed from a bender. As you can tell, I’m Mark. His flatmate Jeremy, a bisexual party-prone maniac, is what keeps Mark’s life fun and interesting, a perfect duo for the “rager” that ensues. 

With half a bottle of gin in my liver, I was feeling quite fine. In fact, it was like I wasn’t drunk at all. Then something happened. Within five minutes I went from “We could probably have sex” to being completely knocked out
