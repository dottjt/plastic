Utter Professionalism

Apparently, I’m a professional.

How cute.

Of course, as a wage earner, I’m also entitled to certain rights over others. The water I drink is of quote “higher quality” than compared to the current crop of non-professional gouging.

While my charming looks sell for a high premium on the European circuit, it’s a tough gig being so fabulous. These jeans… oh wait, I don’t wear jeans anymore.

I just order YOUR type to make them in MY factory so that YOUR blemished children can continue the cycle of “mouth breathers” YOUR kind seem to produce so efficiently.

Take a look at this watch, dearly beloved.

It’s eight carrot gold and I know it hurts you just where your father did before; cunning inside that wallet which keeps you chained to my bank, and it’s no wonder the fees are so exuberant; to tax the ignorant brood, flailing deep inside a system you do not belong.

If you understand the lingo that makes these dollars bounce, then I pray sorely for a dumb lacker like you, to reap some sort of tangible, lower-class sorrow.

I’m a certified professional and here, my friend, the grammar sells itself.

Though don’t worry too much about these words; they’re just a kind of formal eye-candy to keep you entertained.

Everything about me sparkles. I’m like a walking birthday cake; unspoilt as the day you first bought me and took me home. Of course, to your wife.

At first she was shocked.

Then bewildered, though awfully surprised when I fucked her on the kitchen bench.

You see, this is the kind of swindle that emanates from the glow of a seasoned professional.

Those rosy locks, draped like a luscious curl onto the hind of your lap. I slapped her hard, though hard enough? The discerning sincerity was not to be profound.

In retrospect, it was clear we were wonderfully casual.

I’ll have her next time.

There’s a beautiful condition of duplicity playing jazz in my mind; singing like a cheerful plead towards a bigger, juicier prize.

One delicate proposition at a time. The providence is overwhelming; oh, my favourite word. The charm, my dear, and your delightful charmress.

I wouldn’t want to startle you, not while basking in my youth. However, your lips clabber too loudly, like a brash pig urinating on it’s hope for survival.

I’m afraid your pen is not quite big enough to support both your crystal clear failure, nor your delusion. Dumb people are dumb and you, brave idiot, are the least refined of them all.

As the sole recipient of your calamitous stupidity, I dare you to look me in the eye and sunder. Your intrigue is but a pointless dictionary for the appropriately misdirected. The exit awaits your arrival.

These words are flaccid, because the tramp is thin. If you keep this up, you might recieve a big, fat discount on your flat-rate mortgage.

Though I wouldn’t bet on it.

The system refuses to confine.

By Julius Reade. on November 21, 2015.
