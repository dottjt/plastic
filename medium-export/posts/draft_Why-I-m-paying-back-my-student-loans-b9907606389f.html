Why I’m paying back my student loans

Australia is a wonderful country.

Part of what makes our country so agreeable is the affordability of university education, in a way that is immediately accessible and without any real restraints to anyone who is an Australian citizen (I used to sell TAFE education over the phone. The lack of requirements is more-or-less morally fucked.) 

I mean, sure. Our university educaiton is no free lunch in the park, however the laws are incredibly forgiving the point where most people neglect their student loans forever and fail to worry about the consequences. Quite seriously, no one really cares and neither did I.

As is typical of most people, I’d racked up just over $20,000 in debt in the wake of completing my <insert pointless university degree>.

Actually, it was a Bachelor of Journalism. 

Like most art degrees, completely worthless, though in all honesty, valuable in today’s professional world that won’t even consider you unless if you have a piece of paper. 

Listen, if you’re going to get a degree, you may as well get something cheap and durable, to which I have no regrets.

Essentially, it enabled me to land my current writing job in Public Relations and so I suppose it all worked out in the end (though for most part, most people end up working at Grill’d for the rest of their lives). 

I was thinking recently about whether I should pay back my student loans and I couldn’t quite think of a reason why. I mean, the debt only increases roughly 3% each year, which I’m sure you means nothing in the long term (in actual fact, it means that your debt doubles every 23 years, which is terrifying).  

The more I thought about it however, the more I realised that paying it off is probably one of the best things I could be doing. 

It’s not that it’s a financially amazing investment (in many ways, it’s a horrible investment) however for myself, it was more than just creating a piece of mind. 

Put simply, in Australia, I am not legally required to pay back my student loans unless if I earn more than $54,126 per year (with which the requirement increases each year), to which you pay back 4% of your total income towards.

This is essentially why most people don’t pay their loans back. They simply don’t think they are going to earn more than that, which I think is toxic.

The reason why I’m paying back my student loans voluntarily, is because I want to wholly acknowledge that I will be earning more than $54,000 over in the very near future.

When I thought about it this way, paying backing my student loans made sense, more as a motivator for my future.  

Australia is not the easiest place on earth.
