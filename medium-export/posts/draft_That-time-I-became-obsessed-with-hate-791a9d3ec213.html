That time I became obsessed with hate

Hate is like a bubbly chemical that you pour onto your skin.

First you let it simmer, allowing it to become familiar of the way you think and feel, reasoning with your deepest desires; forming intimate bonds with your strings.

Then you let it whisper.

Culminating into a greater melody of erudite ravenousness. Inquisition and arrest.

Your eyes dart endlessly across the room, reverberating from the words you refuse to believe. They echo in your mind. Your teacher as returned home after all these years.

The sinister man is watching you. He’s teaching you fear, accusing you of your own senses.

It hurts to fail, dripping insensate like a leaky deposit of sundered memories. You run into the kitchen, forcing your hand into the draw. With a sharp knife, you scream at the body staring you dead in the eye; covered lawless in unwise blood.

Challenging your menial senselessness, you scrape at your body like a dead man trying to catch his son. It chops and bites and yet the feeling is never quite enough to remove yourself from the hatred eating into you.

You bite deep into your fingers, ripping them off with a single lunge, panting at the exposed bone that you repeated throw into the wall.

The screaming. The stupidity. The hard wall famously bankrupting your attempts at renewed complication, as you hit yourself with a kind of profanity that even the insane never dare enter

The sharp knife shatters into your bone; fragmenting the stainless steel into your supple flesh. You manically slide the broken blade across your tongue, severing it into an array of incomprehensible flesh.

You believe every word he says.

“I” “love” “myself”.

Mindlessly like forcing the words into your head; chaff abruption along the thin river of pain. In a sullen daze; pretending to laugh, pretending to care.

Here’s there, in the distance.

Your pupils immediately shrink, the visceral burn beginning to take over. Bubbling. Crying. A thick reminder to the scars covered deep in your shame.

Do you remember her hanging, dangling like the little piece of shit you are?

You open your mouth and you cry. You cry from the pain, you cry as a way to escape from the laughter you pretended was real.

I will have to stop here.

I don’t think I can write anymore, I’m going insane trying to piece the narrative and trauma together in my head.

I love it, but fuck me.

You see, I have a difficult time feeling. I’m a ragdoll of emotion and I love the challenge of character. I never know quite what to feel and at times, trying to express myself can feel like watching two homeless men beat the shit out of each other.

I need a break.
