Why happiness is so unremarkable

Happiness is nothing special. 

I believe it is a beautiful, entirely wonderful thing to be enjoyed and cherished, however the moment we begin qualifying our emotions as something sacred is when things can get hairy. 

Understandably our sentiment is borne out of good intentions, however it is also a surefire way to begin isolating ourselves from the way we feel and create feeling from within. 

Perhaps the hardest part of overcoming depression is discovering that our emotions are not as they seem, nor significant in dictating who we are.

I don’t mean this in a snark comment to twist the masses. I believe it is a fundamental understanding towards becoming more self-aware, especially of the ways with which we distort our emotions. 

Happiness, like anger, sadness, jealousy and guilt, are all feelings, no different to any other feelings; predicated in the endless series of emotional reactions we all apparently find so *puzzling*. 

The fact that our feelings are unremarkable, however, is an important starting point in recognising and breaking down all the guilt, shame and depression with which we hold with such high regard. 

In our confusion of who we are, we can have a tendency to mysticise our feelings as a platter of dangerous ideological battles; these kind of foreign totems we carry as being inherently unobtainable or even too accessible to the mind.

We overcomplicate the reality of the situation by making all sorts of battered assumptions about what our feelings should be, based and shaped by the experiences we have on a daily basis, rather than simply acknowledging the situation itself. 

Instead of focusing on the fundamentals of what creates happiness, we create an impenetrable fortress around happiness as an image, an action, a criteria we cannot possibly impress. 

We spend so much time churning our minds in these ideological battles that we eventually give up and call defeat. Our mental health clearly cannot be worth anything. Our happiness is just a ruse. 

Ultimately there is nothing special about our feelings or emotions. They do not have some kind of mystical power over us. They are not foreign, unobtainable or dangerous to our health.

You are simply mentally ill, due precisely because you do not understand basic concepts such as these. Your confusion is paramount, however that is precisely what is eating away at you. 

Emotions and feelings are nothing special. They just take a little getting used to, that’s all.
