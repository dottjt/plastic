Something extraordinary happened around two months ago.

My brain clicked.

My neck snapped. My fingers tumbled. My eyes disintegrated. The plastic on my face melted into the seams of my pants and the world was never quite the same again.

And as amazing as that would be to believe, none of this actually happened. Instead, I’d decided to come out to my partner about my mental health.

Certainly, it was an incredibly difficult thing to do. As a fiercely independent person, I’m not the kind of person who likes asking for help. 

My mental health was something that had been bothering me for a while. I think I’d realised that I’d hit the limit in terms of what I could achieve as an individual. And don’t get me wrong, there was so much that I was doing that was right. I was maintaining a consistently healthy diet, I was lifting weights three times a week, I was taking cold showers everyday and I was blah, blah, blah.

Yet, I wasn’t exactly where I wanted to be. I still couldn’t quite beat my porn addiction. I was still finding myself in a place in a regular cycle of mania (even though I’d reduced my cycles significantly) and part of me knew that I could do better. The main thing was that I was hiding my bad habits from my partner, and it’s almost impossible to get better and overcome something if you’re also lying about it to someone. 

I suppose what struck me most was how she didn’t know. She didn’t know I was bipolar and it almost shocked me. I mean, it wasn’t something I’d actively tried to hide — she simply did not know.
