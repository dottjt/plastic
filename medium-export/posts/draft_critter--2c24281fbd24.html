I feel left out of my own mind.

You know, I was going to write a post about cowboys. “Listen cowboy, you have a horse to ride,” and then the scene would transpire into a homoerotic adventure between a man and his ego. You’re in safe hands, sully; crack that whip and hold my hand. We have an adventure awaiting at the steed 901. 

It’s a beautiful Saturday morning. The illiterate fog is making itself home on the window pane glass and the past week’s urgency has made itself sparse, allowing my mind to regather for the communal beating of job interviews and recruiter meet-ups. 

I often ask myself whether it was worth it in the end, getting my shit together and focusing in on my skills. I wonder other kind of life I could have had, with close kin in a dingy share house, uttering wisecrack after wisecrack to a non-paleo brew. For some reason, I feel like it would have felt too much like high school. People think funny people like to be funny, when in reality, merely serves as defence mechanism from the crippling anxiety and awkward stares. 

At home I’m rather quiet. I don’t have much to say, or at the very least, I’ve learnt that it’s better not to have anything to say, to avoid the incessant self-criticism and judgement that comes with my zany observations.
