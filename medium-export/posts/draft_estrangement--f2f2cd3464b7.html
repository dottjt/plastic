Idiot. Idiot. Idiot. 

Admittedly, I’ve always been a late learner. A late, somewhat foolish learner, though a proficient kind of manic once I’ve learnt my lesson. The issue comes down to my learning style. I’m the kind of person that needs to fail in every possible way before I’ll even consider coming to my senses, only to witness the utter destruction that I’ve laid in my path; not to mention the ten dead children I thoughtless killed in a drunken spree with a baseball bat and a bottle of Wild Turkey. 

It’s a particularly destructive and unrelentless process that I would like to formally apologise for, though a necessary process which has helped me grown into the adult I am today. I’m sorry. There is absolutely nothing I can do to help you, though there are always solutions; albeit imperfect, ungradual solutions that eventually make us self-aware enough to realise what crookards we are. 

…and I tell ya what, I really like being a functioning adult or rather, a post-traumatic child. I have this tremendous desire to learn and discover and become the best version of myself. Hell, I haven’t had a fucking soft-drink in years. I’m paleo baby. I have grasp over my porn addiction. I’m go, go, go. For once in my life, I can happily say I’m in control because it actually FEELS happy to have control. 

I don’t believe I deserve a congratulatory smile with a handshake the size of manhattan’s ego. In fact, I’ve really been holding off writing a piece like this, but dare I say, it’s something for me to feel proud of. Of course, the hidden trick is to actually not think about it 

I have a number of strategies that help, though it’s better to call them beliefs, because if you don’t believe in your strategies then you have nothing to hold your hand. The main thing I’ve realised is that having energy is probably the most important thing you can have .
