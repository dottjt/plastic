Riding the wave of rumination 

It’s only once you become self-aware of your destructive habits that it becomes immediately obvious what a fool you’ve been all along.

My own individual self-awareness has also allow me to identify some of the self-destructive behaviours that others embark on, and there is nothing more consequential than rumination.

You know, that sickly little voice in your head that dictates your thoughts and feelings, always repeating the same daunting message which whips you up into an insecure frenzy.

If you ever do work up the courage to overcome your mental illness, which is certainly one of the most difficult and noble things you will ever encounter, then learning to tackle your rumination is probably up there in terms of importance.

Here’s a tip: Stop justifying the narrative in your heads.

When we feel insecure, we become oddly logical to our own detriment. We begin searching for answers to the narrative or story about not being good enough or feeling guilty or ashamed or unbearable of who we are.

“Why do I feel like shit? I must find out why.”

Then inevitably we run ourselves around in circles, exhausted like a dead dog, still fixated on the pointlessness of our existence: simply because we fail to satisfy the big “why” floating above our heads.
